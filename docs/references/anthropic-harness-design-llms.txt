# Anthropic — Harness design for long-running apps (distillate)

Source: anthropic.com/engineering/harness-design-long-running-apps

## Multi-agent specialization
- **Planner**: expands high-level requirements into detailed specs.
- **Generator**: implements features iteratively.
- **Evaluator**: external quality assessment.
- Key failure addressed: agents asked to evaluate their own work confidently
  praise it even when it's obviously mediocre → the evaluator MUST be separate.

## Context: resets vs compaction
- **Resets**: clean slate between sessions; require structured handoff artifacts.
- **Compaction**: summarize in place; preserves continuity but risks "context
  anxiety." (Sonnet 4.5 showed it strongly; newer models reduced it.)

## Sprint contracts (negotiated criteria)
- Generator + evaluator negotiate testable acceptance criteria before building.
- Bridges high-level stories ↔ concrete implementation; avoids over-specifying.

## File-based communication
- Agents pass structured artifacts (files), not just conversation history →
  versioned specs/code/results, clean resets without info loss, clear ownership.

## Evaluation as a design lever
- Make subjective quality gradable: design quality, originality, craft,
  functionality. Calibrate the evaluator with few-shot scored examples.
- Evaluator verifies by **interaction** (e.g. Playwright MCP), not static review.

## Progressive simplification
- "Every component in a harness encodes an assumption about what the model can't
  do on its own — stress-test those assumptions."
- As models improve, REMOVE scaffolding (drop sprint decomposition, single-pass
  the evaluator). The design space shifts, it doesn't shrink.

## Cost/benefit (illustrative)
- Solo agent: ~20 min / $9. Full harness: ~6 h / $200. Tuned: ~3.8 h / $125.
- The evaluator earns its cost when the task is beyond what the model does
  reliably solo.
